Stretched String

In a stretched string fixed at both ends, standing waves form with nodes (no movement) at the ends and antinodes (maximum movement) in between. The fundamental frequency creates one half-wavelength, while higher harmonics create multiple half-wavelengths.

Closed Tube (One End)

In a tube closed at one end, there's a node (no air movement) at the closed end (left) and an antinode (maximum air movement) at the open end (right). Only odd harmonics are possible, creating quarter-wavelength patterns.

Open Tube (Both Ends)

In a tube open at both ends, there are antinodes (maximum air movement) at both ends and nodes in between. Like the string, it supports all harmonics and creates half-wavelength patterns.
