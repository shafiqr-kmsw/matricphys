Experiment: Two Identical Resistors in Series

In this virtual experiment, you will investigate the relationship between current and voltage for two identical resistors connected in series.

When the Generate button is pressed:

  1. A theoretical resistance value (between 3 Ω and 9 Ω) for a single resistor is randomly generated.
  2. An actual resistance value is internally produced with a deviation of less than 10% from the theoretical value.
  3. Using this actual resistance, voltage–current data for two resistors in series is generated.
  4. A best-fit straight line is calculated from the data to determine the effective resistance.
  5. The uncertainty in the gradient and the percentage error relative to the theoretical value are computed.

Assume Ohm’s Law applies and that measurement uncertainty arises from experimental variation only.

Note: Each click produces a new dataset. Record all values before refreshing or regenerating.
